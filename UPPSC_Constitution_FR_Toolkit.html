Preamble — प्रस्तावना

Soul & Mirror of the Constitution
The Preamble of India
WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA, having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a SOVEREIGN SOCIALIST42nd SECULAR42nd DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC

and to secure to all its citizens:

JUSTICE — Social, Economic, Political
LIBERTY — of thought, expression, belief, faith and worship
EQUALITY — of status and of opportunity
FRATERNITY — dignity of individual & unity and integrity42nd of the Nation

IN OUR CONSTITUENT ASSEMBLY this twenty-sixth day of November, 1949
🔴 Red/Orange highlights = Added by 42nd Constitutional Amendment, 1976  |  Preamble amended only once
⚡ SOVEREIGN
India is free from external control. Not subject to any foreign power. Can acquire/cede territory. UN membership doesn't affect sovereignty.
🔴 SOCIALIST (Added 1976)
Democratic Socialism (NOT Communism). Mixed economy. Avoid concentration of wealth. D.S. Nakara v. UoI (1983) — equal pay for equal work.
🔴 SECULAR (Added 1976)
No state religion. Equal respect to all religions. "Positive Secularism" — state can intervene for social reform. Different from Western model.
🗳️ DEMOCRATIC
Govt derives authority from people. Universal Adult Franchise. Includes social & economic democracy via DPSP alongside political democracy.
🏛️ REPUBLIC
Elected head of state (President), NOT hereditary. No monarchy. All public offices open to all citizens through proper process.
🤝 FRATERNITY + INTEGRITY
Brotherhood ensures: (1) Dignity of individual (2) Unity & Integrity of Nation. "Integrity" added by 42nd Amendment 1976.
⚖️

Landmark Cases on Preamble

Berubari Union Case 1960
SC held: Preamble is NOT part of the Constitution; cannot be used to interpret provisions. (LATER OVERRULED!)
Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala 1973 ⭐⭐⭐
🌟 13-judge bench: Preamble IS part of the Constitution. Represents basic structure. Parliament CANNOT amend basic structure. Overruled Berubari case.
S.R. Bommai v. Union of India 1994
Secularism is a basic feature of the Constitution. President's Rule on communal grounds upheld as constitutional.
LIC of India v. Consumer Education & Research Centre 1995
Preamble aids interpretation. Socialism means equal pay for equal work. DPSP and FR should be read harmoniously.
⚠️ EXAM TRAPSCommon Errors on Preamble — MUST KNOW!▼
  • 42nd Amendment (1976) added Socialist + Secular + Integrity HOT MCQ — UPPSC 2025 Q.56
  • Preamble has been amended only ONCE in history (1976)
  • Constitution ADOPTED: 26 Nov 1949 (Samvidhan Divas) | ENFORCED: 26 Jan 1950 (Republic Day)
  • Objectives Resolution moved by Nehru on 13 December 1946
  • "We the People" — CA represented people; no direct referendum was held
  • Justice includes Social, Economic AND Political — all three from Preamble (inspired by USSR Constitution)

K.S. Puttaswamy v. Union of India 2017 ⭐⭐⭐

3. Right Against Exploitation — शोषण के विरुद्ध अधिकार
Art. 23–24
▼
📍 Art. 23 — Traffic & Forced Labour Banned
Prohibits: Human trafficking, Begar (forced labour without pay), other forms of forced labour

Exception: State can impose compulsory service for public purpose (military, social service) — BUT no discrimination on religion/race/caste/class

Available to citizens AND non-citizens
👶 Art. 24 — Child Labour Prohibition
No child below 14 years in any factory, mine, or hazardous employment

Non-hazardous activities NOT covered by Art.24 alone

Child Labour Act 2016 extended ban to age 14 in ALL occupations (with family enterprise exception)

Hazardous = factories, mines, construction
People's Union for Democratic Rights v. Union of India 1982
Asiad workers case. Forced labour = payment below minimum wage also counts. Art.23 includes right to receive minimum wages. Landmark for bonded labour abolition.
🧠 Trick
23 = Trafficking + Begar Banned (adults & children) | 24 = Child below 14 NOT in hazardous work

4. Freedom of Religion — धार्मिक स्वतंत्रता का अधिकार
Art. 25–28
▼
ArtProvisionException/Limit
25Freedom of conscience, practice & propagation"Propagation" ≠ forced conversion (Stainislaus v. State of MP 1977). State can regulate economic activities associated with religion. Can reform Hindu religious institutions.
26Freedom to manage religious affairsSubject to public order, morality, health. Every religious denomination can establish institutions, manage own affairs, own property
27Freedom from taxes for religionNo one compelled to pay tax for promotion of any particular religion. Regulatory fees ARE allowed (not taxes)

Shirur Mutt Case 1954
🌟 "Essential Religious Practices" test: Only essential & integral practices of religion get constitutional protection under Art.25-26. State can regulate secular activities.
Aiyaruddin Navab v. State of MP 1977
Right to propagate does NOT include right to convert by force, fraud, or inducement. Stainislaus case established this limit on Art.25.

5. Cultural & Educational Rights — सांस्कृतिक एवं शैक्षिक अधिकार
Art. 29–30
▼
📖 Art. 29 — Protection of Minority Interests
Any section of citizens with distinct language, script, or culture has right to conserve it

No citizen denied admission to state-aided institution on grounds of religion, race, caste, language

Important: Art.29 applies to ALL citizens (majority & minority both)
🏫 Art. 30 — Minority Right to Establish Institutions
All minorities (religious or linguistic) can establish & administer educational institutions

State shall not discriminate in granting aid to minority institutions

T.M.A. Pai Foundation (2002): Right to establish educational institution is also a FR for all — minorities have additional protection under Art.30
T.M.A. Pai Foundation v. State of Karnataka 2002
11-judge bench. Right to establish educational institution is a FR for all citizens and minorities under Art.19(1)(g) & Art.26 + Art.30. Limits on State's reservation in private/minority institutions.

6. Constitutional Remedies — संवैधानिक उपचार का अधिकार
Art. 32 MOST IMPORTANT — "Heart & Soul"
▼
"Article 32 is the very soul of the Constitution and the very heart of it."
— Dr. B.R. Ambedkar
🔑 What Art.32 Guarantees
Right to move SUPREME COURT directly for enforcement of FRs
SC can issue 5 types of writs
Parliament can empower other courts too (Art.32(3))

Art.226 = HC issues writs (WIDER scope — for any purpose, not just FRs). Both Art.32 and Art.226 are FRs.
📋 Five Writs — HMPCQ
🖐️ Habeas Corpus — Produce the body (illegal detention)
✋ Mandamus — We command (perform public duty)
🤚 Prohibition — Stop inferior court from exceeding jurisdiction
✌️ Certiorari — Transfer/quash order of lower court
👆 Quo Warranto — By what authority (challenge to public office)
🧠 Trick for 5 Writs
HMPCQ = "Hum Mar Phir Chalenge Quickly"
Habeas · Mandamus · Prohibition · Certiorari · Quo Warranto
⚠️ Art.32 CAN be suspended during National Emergency under Art.359. BUT Art.20 & Art.21 CANNOT be suspended. (44th Amendment guarantee)

FR vs DPSP Conflict — Landmark Cases

1951 · Art.31A-B Added
Champakam Dorairajan v. State of Madras
  • SC: FR prevails over DPSP in case of conflict
  • Led to 1st Constitutional Amendment 1951 — added Art.31A & 31B (9th Schedule)
    • 1967 · Golaknath Case
    I.C. Golaknath v. State of Punjab
  • SC: Parliament CANNOT amend Part III FRs at all
  • FRs are transcendental & immutable
  • Led to 24th Amendment (1971) — Parliament CAN amend any part including FRs
    • 1973 · Basic Structure Doctrine ⭐⭐⭐
    Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala
  • 13-judge bench (7:6): Parliament CAN amend Constitution BUT cannot destroy its basic structure
  • Basic structure = Supremacy of Constitution, Republican & Democratic form, Secular character, Separation of powers, Federal character, Free & fair elections, Independence of judiciary
  • Overruled Golaknath but limited Parliament's amending power
    • 1980 · Balance Restored
    Minerva Mills v. Union of India
  • SC struck down 42nd Amendment clauses giving DPSP absolute primacy over FRs
  • Harmony & balance between FR (Part III) and DPSP (Part IV) is basic structure
  • Neither can be given absolute priority over the other
    • 🧠 Memory — FR-DPSP Conflict Cases
    C-G-K-M = Champakam (1951) → Golaknath (1967) → Kesavananda (1973) → Minerva Mills (1980)
    🏛️ UP Angle — Art.40 Panchayati Raj
    Art.40 DPSP was implemented via 73rd Amendment 1992 in Rajiv Gandhi era. UP was among early states to pass the UP Panchayati Raj Act. 3-tier system: Gram Panchayat → Panchayat Samiti → Zila Parishad. Reservation for women (33%), SC/ST/OBC provided under 73rd Amendment.

    • (c) Canada

    ✅ (b) USA — Judicial Review (power of courts to declare laws unconstitutional) is borrowed from the USA. Britain follows Parliamentary Supremacy — courts cannot strike down Acts of Parliament. Canada gave us quasi-federal features; Australia gave Concurrent List.
