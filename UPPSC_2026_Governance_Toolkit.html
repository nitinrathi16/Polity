🗳️ Election Commission of India
🗳️

Election Commission of India

Art. 324

📜 Constitutional Provision

Article 324 — Superintendence, direction and control of elections vested in Election Commission.

Art. 325 — No person to be ineligible for inclusion in electoral rolls on grounds of religion, race, caste or sex.
Art. 326 — Adult Suffrage (18+ years).
Art. 327 — Parliament to make provision for elections.
Art. 329 — Bar on interference by courts.

⚙️ New Appointment Law (2023)

CEC & Other ECs Act, 2023 replaced the 1991 Act.

Selection Committee:
• PM (Chair) • Cabinet Minister nominated by PM • Leader of Opposition (LoS in LS)

Search Committee: Led by Law Minister + 2 senior secretaries (shortlists 5 names).

⚠️ SC directive (2023): Appointment should involve independent mechanism — not just Executive.

👥 Composition

MemberDetail
Chief Election Commissioner1 — Head of ECI, seniority-based elevation
Election CommissionersCurrently 2 — Appointed by President on SC recommendation
Tenure6 years OR age 65, whichever earlier
Salary₹2,50,000/month — Equal to SC Judge — Charged to CFI
RemovalCEC — same as SC Judge (Art. 324). EC — on CEC's recommendation

🌟 Powers & Functions

  • Superintendence, direction & control of elections to Parliament, State Legislatures, President, VP
  • Prepare & revise Electoral Rolls
  • Notify election dates & schedule
  • Recognition of political parties & allot symbols
  • Model Code of Conduct enforcement
  • Advice President/Governor on disqualification of MPs/MLAs
  • Order repoll / countermand election in case of booth capturing
  • Register/de-register political parties
  • Grant/withdraw star campaigner status
  • Set expenditure limits for candidates

📋 Model Code of Conduct (MCC) — Key Points

⚠️ MCC — UPPSC Favorite Topic

• MCC comes into force from the date of announcement of election schedule.
• NOT a statutory document — it is based on consensus among political parties (1960 origin).
• Prohibits: New government schemes, welfare announcements, transfers of officials.
• Ruling party cannot use state machinery for campaign purposes.
• Government advertisements using public funds banned during MCC period.
• ECI can censure, issue show-cause, or approach courts — no direct punitive power under MCC.

🔴 Independence Mechanisms

✅ How ECI Stays Independent

1. CEC removal only through SC Judge-like procedure (Parliament address + Presidential order).
2. Service conditions cannot be varied to CEC's disadvantage after appointment.
3. ECI controls its own secretariat (unlike many other bodies).
4. Election courts cannot be set up without ECI consultation.

⚠️ Limitations

❌ What ECI CANNOT Do

• Cannot regulate election expenditure of political parties (only candidates).
• No power to punish under MCC — depends on executive cooperation.
• Rajya Sabha & State Legislative Council elections — conducted through respective legislatures.
• No jurisdiction over local body elections (done by State Election Commissions).

CAG — Comptroller & Auditor General of India

Art. 148–151

📜 Constitutional Articles

Art. 148 — Establishment of CAG office; appointment by President; tenure; salary = SC Judge.
Art. 149 — Duties and powers (Parliament shall prescribe).
Art. 150 — Accounts of Union & States kept as per CAG's advice.
Art. 151 — CAG's reports laid before Parliament / State Legislature.

Governing Act: CAG's (Duties, Powers & Conditions of Service) Act, 1971.

⚙️ Appointment & Tenure

• Appointed by President of India by warrant under hand & seal.
• Tenure: 6 years OR age 65, whichever earlier.
• Salary: ₹2,50,000/month = SC Judge — Charged to Consolidated Fund of India.
• Ineligible for further office under Government after retirement.
• No prescribed procedure for appointment (Executive decides) — subject to reform debate.

🔴 Removal Process

Proven misbehaviour or incapacity
→
Address by both Houses of Parliament (Special Majority)
→
Presidential Order
Same procedure as removal of a Supreme Court Judge.

📊 What CAG Audits

  • All Union & State government departments
  • Railways, Defence, Post, Telecom
  • 1500+ Public Sector Enterprises
  • 400+ Non-commercial Autonomous Bodies
  • Bodies "substantially financed" by govt (>75%)
  • Panchayati Raj Institutions
  • External audit: IAEA (2022–27), FAO (2020–25)
  • CANNOT audit: RBI, Supreme Court, President's office

⚠️ Key Limitations

❌ Limitations of CAG

• No Comptroller function in India — unlike UK's CAG. Cannot control issue of funds (only audits after expenditure).
• CAG reports laid before legislature — but no power to ensure action on recommendations.
• Delhi govt controversy: 12 CAG reports withheld for 4+ years — CAG has no enforcement power to force tabling.
• Appointment process: No independent committee — solely executive decision (reform debates ongoing).
• No suo motu power to initiate action — only advisory.

📋 UPSC & State PSCs
📋

Union Public Service Commission (UPSC)

Art. 315–323

📜 Constitutional Articles

Art. 315 — UPSC established; State PSC for each state.
Art. 316 — Composition: Chairman + members appointed by President; at least 50% must have held government office for ≥10 years.
Art. 317 — Removal: Only by President on SC inquiry (misbehavior).
Art. 318 — Power to make regulations.
Art. 319 — Prohibition on holding office after retirement (Chairman: no further govt office).
Art. 320 — Functions of UPSC.
Art. 323 — Annual Report to President.

⚙️ Composition & Tenure

• Total 1 Chairman + 10 Members (President decides number).
• Tenure: 6 years OR age 65 years, whichever earlier.
• Salary: ₹2,50,000/month — Charged to CFI.
• No re-appointment after serving full term.
• UPSC Chairman ineligible for any government office after retirement.
• Member can become UPSC Chairman or SPSC Chairman.

🌟 Functions (Art. 320)

  • Conducts examinations for All India Services (IAS, IPS, IFS) and Central Services (Group A & B).
  • Direct recruitment to Central Services posts by interview.
  • Advises on matters related to promotion, transfer, disciplinary matters of civil servants.
  • Advises on principles for appointments, promotions, and transfers.
  • Handles DPC (Departmental Promotion Committee) work.
  • Examines claims for reimbursement of legal expenses by civil servants.

❌ Exclusions (Outside UPSC Purview)

What UPSC Does NOT Handle

• Group C and D posts (handled by Staff Selection Commission, Railways, etc.)
• Posts connected with armed forces
• Posts filled by promotion from ministerial/clerical cadres
• Posts filled based on competitive examination where Parliament/President specifically excludes
• Parliament by law can exclude any service from UPSC purview (Art. 320(3))

⚖️ Independence Mechanisms

✅ How UPSC Stays Independent

1. Removal only by President on SC inquiry — for misbehavior/incapacity.
2. Service conditions fixed by President — cannot be varied to disadvantage post-appointment.
3. Salary charged to CFI — not voted in Parliament.
4. Annual Report to President — laid before Parliament with explanation for non-acceptance.
5. No serving member can practice before UPSC after retirement.

🏛️ State PSC — (UPPSC relevant)

🌟 State PSC (UPPSC)

Art. 315: Each state shall have a PSC.
Art. 316: Chairman & members appointed by Governor.
Removal: By President (not Governor) on SC inquiry.
Governor can suspend a member pending inquiry.

UPPSC:
• Conducts PCS (Provincial Civil Service) exam — SDM, DSP, BDO posts.
• Also conducts RO/ARO, APO, and various other state service exams.
• HQ: Allahabad (Prayagraj), Uttar Pradesh.

🔄 Joint PSC

Art. 315(2): Parliament may establish a Joint PSC for two or more states on request. Members appointed by President. Rarely used in practice.

📄 RTI Act 2005 & CIC

Right to Information Act, 2005

Statutory Act

📜 Background & Art. 19(1)(a)

RTI is derived from Freedom of Speech (Art. 19(1)(a)) — Supreme Court held that right to information is part of this freedom.
Enacted: 15 June 2005, fully effective from 12 Oct 2005.
Replaced: Freedom of Information Act, 2002.
Objective: Promote transparency & accountability in working of public authorities.

⏱️ Time Limits — UPPSC Favorite

Life or liberty of a person involved48 hours
Information obtained from 3rd party40 days (10 extra for 3rd party notice)
First appeal to PIO's superior30 days
First appellate authority decision30 days (extendable to 45)
Second appeal to ICWithin 90 days of first appeal decision
RTI request fee₹10 per application; BPL families exempt

🚫 Exemptions (Section 8) — Critical for Exam

❌ Absolute Exemptions

• Information prejudicial to sovereignty, security, integrity of India.
• Cabinet papers/deliberations
• Information expressly forbidden by court
• Breach of Parliamentary privilege
• Information causing breach of national security

⚡ Qualified Exemptions (Override by Public Interest)

• Commercial confidence / trade secrets
• Information held in fiduciary capacity
• Personal information (no public activity/interest)
• Information that would endanger life of informant
Note: Public interest can override these but NOT absolute exemptions.

Lokpal & Lokayuktas Act, 2013

Statutory Body

📜 Background & Legal Basis

Enacted: 17 Dec 2013 (Parliament), Assent: 1 Jan 2014, Effective: 16 Jan 2014.
Based on recommendations of: 1st ARC (1966), various SC/HC judgments, Anna Hazare movement (2011).
Art. 253: Parliament passed Lokpal Act to give effect to UN Convention on Corruption (ratified 2011).
First Lokpal constituted: March 2019 (after 5 years of Act!).

👥 Composition

MemberDetail
Chairperson1 — Former CJI or Judge of SC
MembersMaximum 8 — at least 50% Judicial, at least 50% from SC/ST/OBC/Minorities/Women
Tenure5 years or age 70, whichever earlier
Appointment byPresident on recommendation of 5-member Selection Committee
Selection CommitteePM (Chair) + Speaker LS + Leader of Opposition LS + CJI + Eminent Jurist
SalaryChair = CJI; Members = SC Judge — Charged to CFI

🎯 Jurisdiction (Who Lokpal Can Investigate)

  • Prime Minister — with conditions: No enquiry into: national security, defence, foreign relations, atomic energy, space; Full Bench required (7 of 8 members); in-camera proceedings
  • Union Ministers
  • MPs (excluding Parliament proceedings covered by Art. 105)
  • Group A, B, C, D officers of Union Govt
  • Employees of Central Public Sector Undertakings
  • Entities receiving ₹10 lakh+ from govt
  • Complaint must be filed within 7 years of commission of offence
  • NOT under Lokpal: Members of Armed Forces, State Governments (Lokayukta for states)

⚠️ Current Challenges

❌ Limitations & Controversies

• No Annual Reports since 2021-22 — accountability deficit.
• Complaints dropped from 2,469 (2022-23) to 233 (2025) — public disillusionment.
• 12-year delay in setting up Prosecution Wing — exposes institutional inertia.
• Depends on CBI for major investigations — CBI itself has independence questions.
• Can only recommend action; cannot directly prosecute.

🔎 CBI / CVC / ED
🔎

CBI — Central Bureau of Investigation

DSPE Act 1946

📜 Legal Basis

Delhi Special Police Establishment (DSPE) Act, 1946.
CBI set up in 1963 on recommendation of Santhanam Committee.
Operates under Ministry of Personnel (not Home).
NOT a Constitutional or Statutory body — created by executive resolution.

⚙️ Key Structure

• Director: Appointed by committee (PM + LoP + CJI) — fixed tenure 2 years under CVC Act.
• Divisions: AC (Anti-Corruption), SC (Special Crimes), BS (Bank Securities Fraud).
• Works under general superintendence of CVC for corruption cases.
• Needs consent of State Govt to investigate in state territory (Section 6 DSPE Act).

⚠️ "Caged Parrot" Controversy

SC in Coal Scam case (2013) called CBI "caged parrot" speaking in its master's voice.
Issue: CBI shares status reports with Law Ministry, which can influence investigation.
SC directed: Status reports to be shared with court, not Ministry, in certain cases.

🔎 Central Vigilance Commission (CVC)

CVC Act, 2003

• Statutory body under CVC Act, 2003.
• Composition: 1 Central Vigilance Commissioner + 2 Vigilance Commissioners.
• Appointment: President on recommendation of PM + Home Minister + LoP (LS).
• Tenure: 4 years or age 65, whichever earlier.
• Function: Superintendence over CBI (anti-corruption work); advises on vigilance matters.
• Exercises general superintendence — cannot direct CBI to file FIR or close case.
• Receives complaints of corruption related to Central Govt employees.

💰 ED — Enforcement Directorate

AspectDetail
Parent LawFEMA 1999 (Foreign Exchange); PMLA 2002 (Money Laundering)
Parent MinistryDepartment of Revenue, Ministry of Finance
PowersArrest, search & seizure, attachment of property, prosecution under PMLA/FEMA
Key FeaturePMLA: Reverse burden of proof — accused must prove innocence regarding laundered assets
ControversiesMultiple opposition leaders arrested; SC scrutiny on ECIR vs FIR equivalence (2022 Vijay Madanlal Choudhary case)

🔄 Inter-relationship

CBI vs CVC vs ED — Key Differences

• CVC: Superintendence body for CBI (anti-corruption); itself investigates top officers.
• CBI: Investigates (anti-corruption, economic offenses, special crimes).
• ED: Enforces economic laws — FEMA, PMLA. NOT under CVC.
• State govts can withdraw CBI consent: Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, Kerala, Maharashtra, Mizoram, Punjab, Rajasthan, Telangana, West Bengal (9 states as of 2025) withdrew general consent.

PHRA 1993

📜 Legal Basis

Protection of Human Rights Act (PHRA), 1993 — amended in 2006 & 2019.
Established: 12 October 1993.
Based on: Paris Principles (1993) — UN standards for national human rights institutions.
NHRC is accredited with A Status by GANHRI (Global Alliance of NHRIs).

🌟 Powers & Functions

  • Inquire into human rights violations suo motu or on complaint
  • Intervene in court proceedings (like an amicus)
  • Visit state-controlled institutions (prisons, etc.) with State Govt approval
  • Review constitutional/legal safeguards for human rights
  • Recommend compensation to victim / action against violator
  • Promote human rights research & education

⚠️ Limitations

❌ Critical Limitations

• Can only recommend — no binding orders.
• Cannot investigate complaints older than 1 year from date of occurrence.
• Armed Forces: Only seeks report from Govt; cannot independently investigate. (Biggest criticism — "toothless against army violations")
• State HRCs have jurisdiction over state-level violations — NHRC refers to them.
• Complaints against courts not entertained.

UP State Election Commission

Art. 243K

📜 Constitutional Provision

Art. 243K (73rd & 74th Amendment 1992): Each state shall appoint a State Election Commissioner.
SEC conducts elections for:
✅ Gram Panchayats, Block Panchayats, Zila Panchayats
✅ Nagar Panchayats, Nagar Palika Parishads
✅ Nagar Nigams (Municipal Corporations)

⚙️ Appointment & Removal

• Appointed by Governor of UP.
• Removal: Same procedure as High Court Judge — by Presidential address.
• Service conditions cannot be varied to disadvantage after appointment.
• UP SEC Established: 23 April 1994.
• HQ: 32, Station Road, Udaiganj, Lalkuan, Lucknow.
• Current SEC: Manoj Kumar, IAS

⚠️ ECI vs UP SEC — Key Difference

• ECI conducts elections for Parliament & State Legislatures (Art. 324).
• UP SEC conducts elections for local bodies only (Art. 243K).
• These are two separate, independent bodies.
• UP SEC decisions are challenged in High Court (Allahabad), not ECI.

🌟 UPPSC (UP Public Service Commission)

UPPSC Key Facts

• Constitutional body under Art. 315.
• Members appointed by Governor; removed by President.
• HQ: Prayagraj (Allahabad).
• Conducts: PCS, PCS-J, ACF/RFO, APO, RO/ARO, and various state service exams.
• Cadre posts directly recruitable by UPPSC: SDM, DSP, District Commandant (Home Guards), etc.
• Chairman appointed by Governor; tenure 6 years or age 62, whichever earlier.

SDM Note: You (PCS aspirant) will be recruited through UPPSC PCS exam and serve under UP's administrative framework — directly managed by UPPSC!

Correct Answers
Which Constitutional Article provides for the Election Commission of India?
  • a) Art. 315
  • b) Art. 324
  • c) Art. 148
  • d) Art. 280
Art. 324 establishes the ECI and vests superintendence, direction and control of elections. Art. 315 = UPSC, Art. 148 = CAG, Art. 280 = Finance Commission.
Who was the FIRST Chief Election Commissioner to be appointed under the CEC & Other ECs (Appointment) Act, 2023?
  • a) Rajiv Kumar
  • b) Gyanesh Kumar
  • c) Sukhbir Singh Sandhu
  • d) Vivek Joshi
Gyanesh Kumar (IAS 1988, Kerala cadre) became CEC on 19 Feb 2025 — first under the new 2023 Act. Born in Agra, UP. Rajiv Kumar preceded him (appointed under old system).
Under Model Code of Conduct (MCC), which of the following is CORRECT?
  • a) MCC is a statutory document enforceable under IPC
  • b) MCC comes into force from the date of election notification
  • c) MCC is a consensual agreement — not a statutory law
  • d) ECI can directly punish violations under MCC
MCC is based on consensus among political parties (started 1960 Kerala). It has no statutory backing and ECI has no direct punitive power — relies on executive cooperation and approaches courts.
The CAG of India as per Article 148 — which of the following is INCORRECT?
  • a) Appointed by the President by warrant under hand and seal
  • b) Salary is charged to the Consolidated Fund of India
  • c) CAG controls issue of funds from the Consolidated Fund — like UK's CAG
  • d) CAG's reports are laid before Parliament under Art. 151
Unlike UK's CAG, India's CAG does NOT control issue of funds — it functions only as Auditor General. India's CAG cannot prevent expenditure — it can only examine it after the fact.
Under RTI Act 2005, information related to life or liberty of a person must be provided within:
  • a) 7 days
  • b) 24 hours
  • c) 48 hours
  • d) 15 days
Section 7(1) of RTI Act: Information involving life or liberty of a person must be provided within 48 hours. Normal requests: 30 days. Third-party information: 40 days.
Which section of RTI Act, 2005 provides for exemptions from disclosure?
  • a) Section 6
  • b) Section 7
  • c) Section 8
  • d) Section 11
Section 8 lists 10 exemptions from mandatory disclosure. Section 6 = procedure for seeking information, Section 7 = disposal of request, Section 11 = third party information.
The current Chairperson of Lokpal (as of 2025-26) is:
  • a) Justice Pinaki Chandra Ghose
  • b) Justice Pradip Kumar Mohanty
  • c) Justice A.M. Khanwilkar
  • d) Justice Ritu Raj Awasthi
Justice Ajay Manikrao Khanwilkar became Lokpal Chairperson on 10 March 2024 (2nd Lokpal). First Lokpal was Justice Pinaki Chandra Ghose (2019-2022). Justice Mohanty served as acting chairperson between them.
Lokpal's jurisdiction over the Prime Minister is subject to which safeguard?
  • a) Prior permission of Rajya Sabha
  • b) Order of Supreme Court
  • c) Full Bench of Lokpal (7 of 8 members) and in-camera proceedings
  • d) PM cannot be investigated under any circumstances
PM can be investigated but with safeguards: Full Bench (7/8 members), in-camera proceedings, matters related to national security/defence/foreign relations/atomic energy/space excluded, CBI need not be involved.
Which Article mandates appointment of State Election Commissioner?
  • a) Art. 324
  • b) Art. 280
  • c) Art. 243K
  • d) Art. 315
Art. 243K (inserted by 73rd & 74th Constitutional Amendment, 1992) mandates appointment of State Election Commissioner for superintendence of local body elections — Panchayats and Municipalities.
Under which Act is the CBI established?
  • a) Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988
  • b) CBI Act, 1963
  • c) Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946
  • d) CVC Act, 2003
CBI operates under the Delhi Special Police Establishment (DSPE) Act, 1946. The CBI itself was set up in 1963 by executive resolution on Santhanam Committee recommendation. There is no "CBI Act" — CBI is not even a statutory body!
NITI Aayog differs from Planning Commission primarily because:
  • a) NITI Aayog is a Constitutional body
  • b) NITI Aayog has no fund allocation powers
  • c) NITI Aayog prepares Five Year Plans
  • d) NITI Aayog Chairman is not the PM
The key functional difference: NITI Aayog has NO powers to allocate funds to states — this is done by Finance Commission. Planning Commission could control funds through Centrally Sponsored Schemes, giving it enormous leverage. Both are executive resolutions; both have PM as Chairman.
The NHRC cannot independently investigate complaints against the Armed Forces. Under Protection of Human Rights Act, what CAN it do?
  • a) Direct court martial proceedings
  • b) Seek a report from the concerned Government
  • c) Order CBI investigation
  • d) Suspend the officer concerned
Section 19 of PHRA: NHRC can only seek a report from Government regarding complaints against Armed Forces — it CANNOT independently investigate. This is the biggest criticism of NHRC.
Which constitutional Article bars courts from interfering in election matters?
  • a) Art. 324
  • b) Art. 326
  • c) Art. 327
  • d) Art. 329
Art. 329 bars courts from questioning validity of electoral laws (Part (a)) and bars election petitions except as provided by law (Part (b)). Election disputes can only be challenged in Election Petitions before High Court after elections.
The CAG's salary is equal to:
  • a) Chief Justice of India
  • b) A Judge of the Supreme Court
  • c) Chief Election Commissioner
  • d) Cabinet Secretary
CAG's salary = SC Judge (₹2,50,000/month). Note: CEC's salary is also = SC Judge. CJI gets more. This salary is charged to Consolidated Fund of India — not voted in Parliament — ensuring independence.
Under which Act is the Enforcement Directorate empowered to attach property related to money laundering?
  • a) FEMA 1999 only
  • b) IPC and CrPC
  • c) Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002
  • d) CVC Act, 2003
ED gets power to arrest, attach property, and prosecute under PMLA 2002. FEMA 1999 deals with foreign exchange violations (civil). ED's key feature: PMLA has reverse burden of proof — accused must prove property is not proceeds of crime.
First Lokayukta in India was established in which state?
  • a) Rajasthan
  • b) Uttar Pradesh
  • c) Maharashtra
  • d) Karnataka
Maharashtra was the first state to establish Lokayukta in 1971. UP established its Lokayukta in 1977 (Justice Vishwambhar Dayal was first). Rajasthan (1973) and Bihar (1974) also preceded UP.
Under UPSC Chairman's post-retirement rules (Art. 319), the Chairman is:
  • a) Eligible to become state PSC Chair
  • b) Eligible for any government appointment
  • c) Ineligible for any further government office
  • d) Eligible to become Election Commissioner
Art. 319: UPSC Chairman is completely ineligible for any further government office after retirement. A UPSC Member (not Chairman) can become UPSC Chairman or a State PSC Chairman. This ensures former UPSC chiefs don't lobby for appointments during their tenure.
Which initiative is described as "UP's one-stop portal for 300+ government services"?
  • a) CM Helpline 1076
  • b) e-Sathee
  • c) IGRS-UP
  • d) Nivesh Mitra
e-Sathee is UP's comprehensive digital service portal for 300+ government services. CM Helpline 1076 = grievance redressal. IGRS-UP = Integrated Grievance Redressal System. Nivesh Mitra = single-window for business investment clearances.
The "caged parrot" remark regarding CBI was made by which Court in which case?
  • a) Allahabad HC in Ayodhya Case
  • b) Delhi HC in 2G Scam case
  • c) Supreme Court in Coal Scam case (2013)
  • d) Supreme Court in Hawala case
In 2013 Coal Scam case, the Supreme Court described CBI as "caged parrot speaking in its master's voice" — criticising the sharing of status reports with government (Law Ministry), which could influence CBI investigations.
Which of the following is NOT a function of NITI Aayog?
  • a) Tracking SDG India Index
  • b) Atal Innovation Mission
  • c) Allocating funds under Centrally Sponsored Schemes to states
  • d) Aspirational Districts Programme
NITI Aayog has NO fund allocation powers — this was the primary change from Planning Commission. Fund allocation (grants, CSS etc.) is done through Finance Commission, Finance Ministry, and line ministries. This is NITI Aayog's biggest limitation.
