Section 1 — Pre-73rd Amendment: Historical Background

🕐 Historical
1957
⚙️ Balwant Rai Mehta Committee
Recommended: 3-tier Panchayati Raj system — Gram Panchayat (village), Panchayat Samiti (block), Zila Parishad (district).
Key concept: "Democratic Decentralisation" — first formal recommendation for PRIs.
Rajasthan first implemented it (2 Oct 1959, Nagaur district), followed by AP.
1977
🏗️ Ashok Mehta Committee
Recommended: 2-tier system — Zila Parishad (district) + Mandal Panchayat (group of villages, 15,000-20,000 pop).
Key features: Official political party participation at village level; Nyaya Panchayats abolished; SC/ST reservation.
Rejected: 3-tier dropped in favour of 2-tier — NOT implemented.
1985
📋 G.V.K. Rao Committee
Called Panchayati Raj "a grass without roots" — bureaucratisation of development had undermined PRIs. Recommended: District as basic unit; Zila Parishad should be apex body; regular elections.
1986
🔬 L.M. Singhvi Committee
First to recommend Constitutional status to PRIs.
Coined: "Gram Sabha" as foundation of democracy.
Recommended: Nyaya Panchayats for minor disputes; Village clusters for viability.
1989
❌ 64th/65th Constitutional Amendment Bills — Lapsed
Rajiv Gandhi govt introduced these bills to give PRIs/ULBs constitutional status. Passed in Lok Sabha, rejected in Rajya Sabha. Eventually led to 73rd/74th Amendment in 1992.
1992
✅ 73rd Constitutional Amendment Act — LANDMARK
Passed by Parliament; received President's assent on 20 April 1992; came into force 24 April 1993.
Added Part IX (Art. 243-243O) + 11th Schedule (29 subjects).
24 April is celebrated as Panchayati Raj Diwas 🎉

🧠 Trick to Remember Committees

Balwant (3-tier, 1957) → Ashok (2-tier, 1977) → GVK (grass without roots, 1985) → LM Singhvi (Constitutional status, 1986)
Mnemonic: BAGL — "Bharat ka Aadhar Gram Level hai"

🎯 Reservation Provisions (Art. 243D)

  • SC/ST seats: Proportionate to their population in that area
  • Women: Not less than 1/3rd of total seats including SC/ST reserved seats
  • OBC: Left to state legislature's discretion (not mandatory)
  • Chairpersons: Also reserved for SC/ST & Women (1/3rd)
  • Rotation: Reserved seats rotate each election
  • UP Special: UP provides 50% reservation for women (above constitutional minimum)

🏦 State Finance Commission (Art. 243-I) — KEY FACTS

  • Constituted by Governor after every 5 years
  • Reviews financial position of Panchayats & makes recommendations to Governor
  • Recommends: Tax distribution between state & PRIs; Grants-in-aid from Consolidated Fund
  • State shall place SFC report before State Legislature
  • UP SFC: UP has constituted multiple SFCs; 5th SFC recommendations under consideration for UPPSC 2026

🗳️ State Election Commission (Art. 243K)

  • SEC is completely independent of Election Commission of India
  • State Election Commissioner has same status as HC Judge — removal same procedure
  • SEC controls: Superintendence + Direction + Control of ALL local body elections
  • Cannot be removed except by impeachment-like procedure
  • UP SEC: Based in Lucknow; currently overseeing 2026 Panchayat election preparation

🏡 Gram Panchayat — Key Provisions (UP Act)

  • Population basis: Minimum 1000 population for a Gram Panchayat
  • Ward Members: 9-15 ward members (varies by pop)
  • Gram Pradhan: Elected directly by voters, 5-year term
  • Up-Pradhan: Deputy — elected by ward members
  • Meetings: Gram Sabha meets at least 2 times/year (UP provision)
  • Quorum: 1/5th of total Gram Sabha members
  • Panchayat Secretary: Government-appointed, does not vote
  • No confidence: Can be removed by 2/3 majority after 2.5 years

🏢 Kshetra Panchayat — Key Provisions

  • Coverage: One block = one Kshetra Panchayat
  • Pramukh: Indirectly elected (by ward members + ex-officio members)
  • Ex-officio members: All Gram Pradhans + MLAs/MLCs/MPs of area
  • BDO: Chief Executive Officer of Kshetra Panchayat
  • Functions: Secondary education, minor irrigation, small industries

🏛️ Zila Panchayat — Key Provisions

  • Adhyaksha: President, indirectly elected
  • CEO: District Magistrate or IAS-level officer
  • Members: Directly elected + ex-officio (MPs/MLAs/MLCs)
  • Functions: Higher education, district roads, district hospitals

Section 6 — 74th Amendment (1992): Urban Local Bodies

Art. 243P–243ZG

📅 Key Dates

  • Passed: 74th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1992
  • Assent: 20 April 1992
  • Force: 1 June 1993
  • Part IX-A added: Art. 243P–243ZG
  • 12th Schedule: 18 subjects for ULBs
  • Note: 73rd & 74th came together, implemented simultaneously

📜 Key Articles (74th Amendment)

  • Art. 243P: Definitions (Municipality, Ward, Transitional area)
  • Art. 243Q: Constitution of Municipalities
  • Art. 243R: Composition of Municipalities
  • Art. 243S: Ward Committees (for cities > 3 lakh pop)
  • Art. 243T: Reservation of seats (SC/ST + 1/3 women)
  • Art. 243U: Duration (5 years)
  • Art. 243V: Disqualification
  • Art. 243W: Powers & Functions (12th Schedule)
  • Art. 243X: Finance (taxes, fees, grants)
  • Art. 243Y: Finance Commission
  • Art. 243Z: Audit
  • Art. 243ZA: Elections (SEC)
  • Art. 243ZG: Bar to interference by courts

🏙️ Three Types of Urban Local Bodies

🌱 Nagar Panchayat

Population: Transitional area — urban & rural character
Pop range: Approx 12,000–25,000 (varies by state)
Head: Adhyaksha (President)
Status: Smallest ULB, for fast-growing towns
Art: 243Q(1)(a)
UP count: ~438 Nagar Panchayats

🏘️ Nagar Palika Parishad

Population: Smaller urban area
Pop range: Approx 25,000–5,00,000
Head: Adhyaksha (President) + EO
Also called: Municipal Council / Nagar Palika
Art: 243Q(1)(b)

🏛️ Nagar Nigam

Population: Larger urban area
Pop range: Generally > 5 lakh
Head: Mayor (elected) + Nagar Ayukt (IAS Commissioner)

⚡ Ward Committees (Art. 243S) — UPPSC Trick Question

Mandatory only for cities/towns with population of 3 lakh or more. Ward Committee consists of one or more wards within a Municipality. Legislature may make provisions for constitution and functions of Ward Committees. ⚠️ Often confused — 3 lakh, NOT 1 lakh

Residuary powers with Parliament (unlike USA where with states)

RS can empower Parliament to legislate on State List (2/3 majority)
Art. 250

Parliament can legislate on State List during Art. 352 Emergency
Art. 252

Parliament can legislate on State List if 2+ states request
Art. 253
International Treaties
Parliament can legislate on any subject for international obligations
Art. 256
State's Obligation
States must comply with laws of Parliament
Art. 257
Union Control over States
Union can give directions to states in matters of national importance
Art. 262
Inter-State Water Disputes
Parliament can set up tribunal; HC/SC jurisdiction excluded
Art. 263
Inter-State Council
President can establish ISC for coordination between Centre-State
Art. 280
Finance Commission
President constitutes FC every 5 years for Centre-State financial distribution

🏗️ Inter-State Council (Art. 263)

  • Set up by President's Order 1990 (Sarkaria Commission recommendation)
  • Chair: Prime Minister
  • Members: All Chief Ministers + 6 Cabinet Ministers from Union
  • Meets periodically; advisory body for Centre-State coordination
  • NOT a permanent constitutional body — established by executive order

🗺️ Zonal Councils

  • Created under States Reorganisation Act 1956 (statutory, NOT constitutional)
  • 5 Zones: Northern, Southern, Eastern, Western, Central
  • Chair: Union Home Minister (all 5 councils)
  • UP in: Central Zone (with MP, Chhattisgarh, Uttarakhand)
  • Advisory bodies — no binding power
  • NE Council: Separate body under NE Council Act 1971

Section 10 — SDM Topper: Must-Remember Facts & Traps

⭐ High Yield

⚠️ Common UPPSC Traps

  • 73rd Amendment came into force 24 April 1993, NOT when passed (20 April 1992)
  • Gram Sabha ≠ Gram Panchayat. Sabha = body of voters; Panchayat = executive body
  • 11th Schedule: 29 subjects, 12th Schedule: 18 subjects
  • Ward Committees mandatory for pop ≥ 3 lakh (NOT 1 lakh)
  • Zonal Councils are statutory (States Reorganisation Act), NOT constitutional
  • Inter-State Council (Art. 263) is set up by Executive Order, not automatically
  • 73rd Amendment applies to states; Nagaland, Meghalaya, Mizoram EXEMPT (Art. 243M)
  • Finance Commission ≠ State Finance Commission. SFC = for local bodies only

📌 UP-Specific Must-Know

  • UP: 50% women reservation in Panchayats (constitutional min = 33%)
  • UP has 17 Nagar Nigams including Ayodhya
  • UP Panchayat Election 2026: 57,965 GPs + 826 Blocks + 75 Districts
  • UP SEC headquarters: Lucknow
  • UP Gram Pradhan term: 5 years; removal after 2.5 years by 2/3 majority
  • UP: Kshetra Panchayat = block level (NOT Panchayat Samiti in UP terminology)
  • UP: Zila Panchayat Adhyaksha = indirectly elected
  • 24 April = Panchayati Raj Diwas 🎉
